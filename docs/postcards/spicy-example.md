# Spicy Postcard Example

A 3-player Campfire game where promises got tested. One player
broke two promises and still almost won.

```
╭─────────────── Campfire Postcard ───────────────╮
│  Sovereignty: Campfire                          │
│  Round 10 | Fox, Gem, Hawk                      │
│                                                 │
│  Fox: 15c 1r 3u | Gem: 9c 6r 2u | Hawk: 7c 8r 1u │
│                                                 │
│  Proof: sha256:e7b2d9...                        │
│                                                 │
│  Ouch: Fox broke their promise: "help Gem" -2r  │
│  Ouch: Fox broke their promise: "no stealing" -2r│
│  Trade: Hawk offers 3 coins for Fox's loyalty    │
╰───────── sov postcard --style spicy ─────────────╯
```

**What happened:** Fox went full Prosperity and didn't care about
reputation. Two broken promises dropped Fox to 1 Rep — but 15 coins
and 3 upgrades meant a score of 18.5. Gem played it straight and
scored 16.5. Hawk tried to broker peace and scored 14.5. Fox won by
being shameless. The table had opinions.

**Why this postcard works:** The spicy style surfaces conflict.
Two broken promises in one game is unusual and tells the story of
a player who decided reputation wasn't worth the cost. The trade
offer from Hawk shows someone trying to buy what they couldn't earn.
