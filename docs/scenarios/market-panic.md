# Market Panic

> The shelves are emptying. Prices are climbing. Somebody just bought
> all the wood and they're not sharing. This is the session where the
> market has moods — and those moods shape everything. Hoard or sell.
> Trade or hold. The economy doesn't care about your feelings.

| Setting | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Tier | Town Hall |
| Recipe | market |
| Players | 3-4 |
| Rounds | 10-12 |
| Time | 45-60 min |

## What to expect

The market recipe loads the deck with supply shocks: Bumper Harvest
floods food, Logging Ban squeezes wood, Warehouse Fire burns through
stockpiles, and Drought shuts the market entirely. Town Hall's scarcity
pricing means every supply event moves prices in real time.

This is the scenario where resources matter more than coins. Players
who learn to read the market — buying when supply is high, selling
before a crash — will pull ahead. Players who hoard create scarcity
for everyone else.

Watch for the first time a resource hits supply 2. That's when prices
spike and the table energy shifts. Also watch for the first
resource-based Offer: "I'll trade you 1 Wood for 3 coins."

## Start command

```bash
sov new --tier town-hall --recipe market -p Alice -p Bob -p Carol -p Dave -s 3
```

## After the game

Run `sov postcard --style economic` to highlight market mood and prices.

Debrief question: *"Did anyone see the crash coming? What would you
do differently?"*
